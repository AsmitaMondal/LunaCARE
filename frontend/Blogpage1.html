Menstrual Cups: Questions, Myths, and Misconceptions

What is a menstrual cup?

A menstrual cup is a small, flexible, funnel-shaped cup made of silicone or latex rubber. Instead of absorbing your flow, like a tampon or pad, it catches and collects it. Cups can hold more blood than other methods, leading many women to use them as an eco-friendly alternative to tampons. Depending on your flow, you can wear a cup for up to 12 hours.

How do I use a menstrual cup?

First, you should wash your hands. Then, you’ll fold the menstrual cup tightly and insert it like a tampon without an applicator. If properly inserted, you shouldn’t feel it. Your cup will spring open inside your vagina, and rest against the walls of your vagina. It’ll create a seal to prevent leaks. You’ll remove your cup by pulling on the stem or pinching the base to release the seal.

Can a menstrual cup get stuck?

No, it’s physically impossible for a menstrual cup to get lost in your vagina. Your vagina ends at your cervix, which blocks the cup from moving further into your body. If you have trouble removing your cup, stay calm. Relax your muscles and gently tug on the stem while bearing down. You can also pinch the base of the cup to release the suction seal, making it easier to remove.

Is a menstrual cup better than tampons?

Both menstrual cups and tampons have their pros and cons. Menstrual cups are more environmentally friendly, as they can last for years with proper care. Tampons are single-use products that contribute to landfill waste. Cups can also hold more blood, which may be beneficial for people with heavy flows. However, some may find tampons easier to use, especially if they’re new to menstrual cups.

Do menstrual cups hurt?

If used correctly, menstrual cups shouldn’t hurt. It may take a little practice to insert and remove the cup without discomfort. Some users might experience minor irritation or a sensation of fullness. If you feel pain while wearing a cup, it’s possible that the cup is not inserted properly, or you may need to try a different size or brand.

Can I use a menstrual cup if I have an IUD?

Yes, you can use a menstrual cup if you have an IUD, but you should be cautious. When removing your cup, make sure to break the suction seal before pulling it out. Pulling out the cup without breaking the seal can cause the IUD to move or dislodge. Consult with your healthcare provider for personalized advice if you have concerns.

Can a menstrual cup cause toxic shock syndrome (TSS)?

There have been very few cases of TSS associated with menstrual cups. TSS is a rare but serious condition caused by bacterial infection. It’s important to follow the instructions for cleaning and sterilizing your cup to reduce the risk. Remember to remove and empty your cup every 4 to 12 hours, depending on your flow.

How do I clean a menstrual cup?

You should clean your menstrual cup by washing it with mild, unscented soap and water every time you remove it. At the end of your period, you should sterilize it by boiling it in water for a few minutes. Make sure the cup is completely dry before storing it in a breathable container, such as a cotton bag.

Are there different sizes of menstrual cups?

Yes, menstrual cups come in different sizes. The size you need depends on your age, whether you’ve given birth, and the strength of your pelvic floor muscles. Smaller sizes are typically recommended for people under 30 who haven’t given birth. Larger sizes are often recommended for those over 30 or who have given birth vaginally.

Can I use a menstrual cup overnight?

Yes, you can use a menstrual cup overnight. Menstrual cups can be worn for up to 12 hours, so you won’t need to wake up during the night to change it, unlike with tampons or pads. Just make sure to empty and clean your cup before bed and again when you wake up.

How long can I use a menstrual cup before replacing it?

With proper care, a menstrual cup can last for several years, typically around 1 to 2 years, though some may last up to 10 years. Inspect your cup regularly for signs of wear, such as cracks or a sticky texture. If you notice any damage, it’s time to replace it.

Conclusion

Menstrual cups are a sustainable, cost-effective alternative to traditional menstrual products. While they may require some practice and adjustment, they offer many benefits, including longer wear time and less environmental impact. Always consult with a healthcare provider if you have any concerns or questions about using a menstrual cup.
